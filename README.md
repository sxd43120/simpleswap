## What SimpleSwap Actually Does

SimpleSwap is what the industry calls a swap aggregator. Instead of running its own order book, it routes your trade through a network of liquidity providers and partner exchanges, finds you a rate, and sends the converted crypto straight to the wallet address you specify. At no point does SimpleSwap hold your coins — you send, they route, your destination wallet receives.

The practical experience is about as frictionless as crypto gets:

1. Select the coin you're sending and the coin you want to receive
2. Enter the destination wallet address
3. Send your crypto to the deposit address they give you
4. Wait a few minutes

That's it. No account required for a basic swap. No registration. No waiting for email verification before you can do anything.

The coin selection is legitimately impressive — over 1,500 cryptocurrencies and tokens are supported. Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, Monero, obscure altcoins, stablecoins, the whole spectrum. Fiat-to-crypto is also available through a partnership with Mercuryo, though that does require ID verification (as fiat ramps generally do by regulation).

## The Non-Custodial Design and What It Means for Security

Since SimpleSwap never holds your assets — funds move from your wallet to the liquidity routing layer to your destination wallet — there's nothing stored on their servers for a breach to expose. Your private keys stay with you. That's a structurally different security posture than centralized exchanges where your coins sit in a platform wallet.

Accounts support two-factor authentication for added protection. The biggest security variable with SimpleSwap, practically speaking, is making sure you're entering the correct destination wallet address — since crypto transactions are irreversible, the usual best practices apply.
